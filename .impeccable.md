## Design Context

### Users
Multiple audiences share the same space depending on which note they land on:
- **Developers & engineers** — following a link, reading at a desk, looking for technical depth
- **Curious readers** — drawn to the mix of technical and philosophical content, browsing the garden
- **Recruiters / potential employers** — gauging personality, taste, and competence before or after outreach

They arrive in different modes but share one expectation: a calm, focused reading experience where the content is the reason for being there.

### Brand Personality
**Quiet · Thoughtful · Direct**

Precise and crafted, warm but no fluff. A calm intellectual space with personality. Not corporate, not performative — the site of someone who thinks carefully, writes honestly, and has genuine taste. Technical rigor and human warmth coexist without one undermining the other.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Dark-only** — Obsidian Baseline theme, deliberately desaturated. No colored accents competing for attention; gray (#999/#b3b3b3) is the entire accent language.
- **Editorial typography** — Instrument Serif for headings (literary, distinctive), Instrument Sans for body (clean, modern), JetBrains Mono / Commit Mono for code (precise, purposeful).
- **Design that recedes** — the interface should feel invisible while in use. Navigation, sidebars, and UI chrome exist to serve, not to impress.
- **Warm but not casual-cute** — the voice is approachable ("Lo there traveler") but the design isn't playful or decorative. Warmth comes from content and copy, not from visual ornament.
- **Crafted in the details** — spacing, type scale, interaction states done deliberately. Visible effort in the small things.

### Design Principles
1. **Content is the star** — every layout, color, and spacing decision should serve readability and focus. If it competes with the text, it loses.
2. **Restraint is a feature** — the desaturated palette and quiet UI are intentional. Adding more color, animation, or decoration would be a step backward.
3. **Warmth through voice, not visuals** — personality lives in copy and casual phrasing. The design stays calm.
4. **Crafted details, consistent subtlety** — small things done right compound. Hover states, focus rings, spacing rhythm, transition timing — these matter.
5. **Serve all three audiences at once** — the site must feel credible to a recruiter, intellectually honest to a developer, and welcoming to a casual reader. None of these should feel like an afterthought.
